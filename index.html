Pokémon Arena! is a head-to-head battle game inspired by games like Dice Throne.
Players select a Pokémon and use attacks, defenses, and strategy to reduce their opponent’s HP to 0.
The game supports multiplayer and team formats, but this rulebook focuses on 1v1 battles.

Each player:

  • Chooses 1 Pokémon
  • Uses a unique deck
  • Builds their stats over time
  • Uses attacks and defenses to outplay their opponent




Each player selects one Pokemon to play as:

  • Charizard (Fire/Flying)
  • Pikachu (Electric)
  • Exeggutor (Grass/Psychic)
  • Greninja (Water/Dark)
  • Alolan Ninetales (Fairy/Ice)
  • Gengar (Ghost/Poison)
  • Lucario (Fighting/Steel)
  • Garchomp (Ground/Dragon)

Each Pokemon has a character mat, which includes:

  • Passive ability
  • Description
  • Type
  • Innate attack
  • Slots for 4 attack moves and 2 defense moves




Health (HP)

  • Recommended starting HP: 40
  • Maximum HP: Starting HP + 10

Starting Resources

  • 5 Energy Points (EP)
  • 5 Ability Points (AP)

Starting Setup

  • Draw 5 cards
  • You may place:
    • 1 attack move (free)
    • 1 defense move (free)





Each Pokémon has its own deck consisting of:

  • 20 Attack Moves
    • 10 Attack (Atk)
    • 10 Special Attack (Sp Atk)
  • 10 Defense Moves
    • 5 Defense (Def)
    • 5 Special Defense (Sp Def)
  • 30 Item Cards

Card Rules

  • You may place 1 new move per turn
  • Placing a move costs 3 EP
  • You can have:
    • Up to 4 attack moves
    • Up to 2 defense moves
  • Cards that are already on the mat cannot be sold. They can only be discarded.
  • Cards from your hand can be sold for 1 EP and 1 AP each
  • Cards that are discarded from your mat or if they were sold, go into the discard pile face up
  • If you run out of cards, reshuffle your discard pile and use that as your new deck
  • You can have a maximum of 5 cards in your hand at a time
    • When your turn is over, you must end it with 5 or less cards in your hand




These are your two main resources:


Energy Points (EP)

Used for:

  • Placing moves (3 EP)
  • Drawing extra moves (5 EP)
  • Refining (see below)
  • Leveling

Ability Points (AP)

Used for:

  • Performing attacks
  • Using defense
  • Activating move abilities
  • Leveling up

Gaining Resources


During your Upkeep Phase:

  • Gain 5 EP
  • Gain 3 AP
  • Draw 1 card

You may also gain EP/AP through:

  • Dealing damage (Grants 1 EP)
  • Placing moves (Grants 2 AP)
  • Buffs or abilities

Refining


Refining lets you filter your deck:

  • Look at the top 3 cards
  • Choose 1 to keep
  • Place the other 2 on the bottom




Each turn follows this order:

  1. Upkeep Phase
    • Resolve status effects
    • Gain 5 EP and 3 AP
    • Draw 1 card
  2. Main Phase
  3. You may perform any of the following:
    • Place a move (3 EP)
    • Draw an extra card (5 EP)
    • Refine (7 EP)
    • Sell a card (gain 1 EP and 1 AP)
  1. Attacking Phase
  2. (See Attacking below)
  1. Defensive Phase
  2. (Occurs during your attack. See Defending below)








Overview Stats represent how your Pokémon enhances or resists attacks beyond the base power of moves. There are four stats: Attack (Atk) Special Attack (Sp Atk) Defense (Def) Special Defense (Sp Def) Each stat ranges from 0 to 6. Stat Thresholds Stats do not directly add or subtract damage per point. Instead, they provide bonuses at specific thresholds: Attack / Special Attack 0–1 → +0 damage 2–3 → +1 damage 4–5 → +2 damage 6 → +3 damage (maximum) Defense / Special Defense 0–1 → -0 damage 2–3 → -1 damage 4–5 → -2 damage 6 → -3 damage (maximum) How Stats Work Attack (Atk) increases bonus damage for Attack-type moves Special Attack (Sp Atk) increases bonus damage for Special Attack-type moves Defense (Def) reduces bonus damage from Attack-type moves Special Defense (Sp Def) reduces bonus damage from Special Attack-type moves Stats only affect bonus damage, not the base damage listed on cards. Attack stats are always compared against the matching defense: Atk vs Def Sp Atk vs Sp Def Key Rules Stats never reduce base damage Maximum bonus from stats is +3 / -3 Stats are applied before defense cards reduce damage If damage would be reduced to 0 or less, it instead deals 1 damage, provided the user’s relevant attacking stat (Attack or Special Attack) is at least 2.




Attacking happens during the Attacking Phase of your turn. You are not required to attack. Each Pokémon has an Innate Attack, which requires no AP to use but is very weak. It will always deal 1 damage. Tokens can modify it. You may only attack once per turn. Typing When attacking, compare the type of your attack move to the type of the opponent’s defense move. Enhanced (type advantage): +2 damage Weakened (type disadvantage): -2 damage Neutral: no change This system is called Typing. Examples: Fire attack vs Grass defense → Enhanced (+2) Fire attack vs Water defense → Weakened (-2) Fire attack vs Electric defense → Neutral Attacking Details Attacks require AP, listed on the card Stronger moves typically cost more AP If you don’t have enough AP (or choose not to spend it), you may use your Innate Attack instead Buffs and debuffs are applied before damage is calculated Status effects are applied after damage is dealt You may only use one attack per turn Overview Attacking occurs during the Attacking Phase of your turn. You may choose to attack once per turn. Each attack uses either: Attack (Atk) or Special Attack (Sp Atk) Attack Steps To perform an attack, resolve the following steps in order: 1. Declare Attack Choose one attack move from your active moves Pay the required AP cost 2. Opponent Defends The defending player selects one of their defense cards They pay the required AP cost 3. Calculate Base Damage Start with the attack’s listed damage 4. Apply Typing Enhanced: +2 damage (type advantage) Weakened: -2 damage (type disadvantage) Neutral: no change 5. Apply Stat Bonuses Determine which stats are used: Attack moves use Atk vs Def Special Attack moves use Sp Atk vs Sp Def Apply stat thresholds: Add attacker’s bonus Subtract defender’s bonus 6. Apply Defense Card Subtract the amount blocked by the defense card 7. Final Damage Deal the remaining damage to the defending Pokémon’s HP If damage is 0 or less, deal 1 damage instead 8. Resolve Effects Apply any status effects, buffs, or debuffs listed on the move




Defending occurs when your opponent attacks you. You may use one of your active defense cards to reduce incoming damage. Defending Rules You may use 1 defense card per attack Defense cards require AP to use You must choose a defense card after the attack is declared Strategy Notes When defending, consider: The type of the incoming attack (for Typing advantage) Whether the attack is: Atk → use Def Sp Atk → use Sp Def Choosing the correct defense card can significantly reduce damage. Defense Resolution During an attack: 1. Choose a defense card 2. Spend the required AP 3. Apply Typing (handled during attack calculation) 4. Subtract the defense card’s block value 5. Remaining damage is applied to your HP




All Pokémon start at Level 0 Max level is 8 Spend 10 EP + 10 AP to level up Heal 5 HP (cannot exceed max HP cap) upon a level up Upon level up, levels 1–4 → gain 1 stat point Upon level up, levels 5–8 → gain 2 stat points Total stat points at max level: 12 Each stat caps at 6 Choose carefully—you cannot max everything
